# Skill: Google Search Ad Launch

## What It Is For

Targeting high-intent search keywords on Google Search. People searching for exactly what you sell. Higher buyer intent than any other platform. The trick: find the keywords your competitors are not bidding on and own them at a fraction of the cost.

## When to Use It

When your customers search Google to find what you sell and you want to appear at the top for those searches.

## The Strategy

Obvious keywords are expensive because every competitor bids on them. The strategy: target the long tail, the specific, the niche. Keywords with lower monthly volume but much higher commercial intent and dramatically lower CPC.

**Example:** "hire developers" = competitive, expensive. "hire remote TypeScript developers for startup" = lower volume, lower cost, higher intent from exactly your buyer.

## The Launch Prompt

```
I want to launch Google Search ads for [product]. My target customer is [describe]. 

Help me:
1. Research keyword opportunities — focus on long-tail keywords with high buyer intent and lower competition
2. Build a keyword list: exact match for high-intent terms, phrase match for research terms, negative keywords to exclude irrelevant traffic
3. Write 3 ad headline variations and 3 description variations (Google tests combinations automatically)
4. Design the campaign structure: campaigns, ad groups, keyword groupings
5. Set up conversion tracking via Google Tag Manager
6. Set the initial budget and bidding strategy
7. Define the optimization schedule for the first 60 days

Walk me through each step.
```

## Keyword Research Approach

Use Google Keyword Planner to identify:
- High-intent long-tail keywords (3-5 words) under $10 CPC
- Competitor brand keywords (if legal in your market)
- Problem-first keywords ("how to hire remote developers fast")
- Job-to-be-done keywords ("find vetted developers quickly")

## Quality Score = Lower CPC

Google rewards relevance. If your keyword, ad copy, and landing page all clearly address the same thing, your Quality Score goes up and your CPC goes down. Align all three tightly.

## What Done Looks Like

- Campaign live with 3+ ad groups organized by keyword intent
- Conversion tracking confirmed in Google Ads dashboard
- Negative keyword list preventing irrelevant spend
- First 14 days: do not optimize, collect data
- Day 15+: pause keywords with 0 conversions after 100 clicks, scale keywords converting below target CPA
