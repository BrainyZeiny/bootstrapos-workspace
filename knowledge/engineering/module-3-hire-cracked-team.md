# Module 3 — Hire and Manage a Cracked Remote Team for Cheap

## What This Module Covers

The biggest lie in startups is that great engineering talent is expensive. It is not. It is just concentrated in the wrong places.

Through RocketDevs, over 300,000 developers have been interviewed and vetted. 90,000 vetted per year. 98% success rate. 2% refund rate. This module gives the complete playbook for building a world-class remote engineering team at a fraction of what funded competitors pay.

Lessons cover:

1. **The limits of vibe coding** — Every LLM has a context window. Your codebase will eventually outgrow it. Knowing when to hire a developer — and why you should hire so you can focus on sales and marketing — is one of the most important decisions you make.

2. **The mistakes that cost founders thousands** — Agency horror stories. Losing ownership of your code. Thinking you do not need to be involved. Upwork without accountability. What actually works.

3. **Where to hire from** — After interviewing 300,000 developers across dozens of countries: good developers come from everywhere. Geography is irrelevant. Skills are everything. AI-forward is non-negotiable.

4. **The two vetting questions** — The exact system used at RocketDevs. Two questions that any non-technical founder can administer. The Jira board test for frontend. LeetCode medium problems for backend. No coding knowledge required to evaluate the results.

5. **What to actually pay** — Realistic ranges for vetted remote developers at different skill levels. How to think about bang for buck.

6. **How to hire and manage** — The developer who gives a shit vs the brilliant developer who does not care. Daily stand-ups. Office hours. Time accountability. The system that produces great products and long-term relationships.

## Key Principles

**Hire when:**
- The codebase has outgrown what Claude handles cleanly
- You have revenue and time is the bottleneck
- You need your head up to focus on marketing and sales

**Do not hire when:**
- You have no revenue yet
- Claude can still handle what you need
- You have not gotten your first paying customer

**The two vetting questions:**
1. Build me a Jira board (live, in front of you) — evaluates frontend by eye
2. Two medium LeetCode problems (live, LeetCode runs the tests) — evaluates backend without requiring you to read code

**Management system:**
- Daily 10-minute stand-ups
- Office hours (working sessions, not status updates)
- Time tracking from day one — transparency creates trust
- Stay involved in product decisions every day
