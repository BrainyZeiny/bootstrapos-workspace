# Code of Conduct

## The Short Version

Be kind. Be helpful. Be the kind of person you would want to collaborate with.

BootstrapOS is a community of founders building real businesses. We are here to help each other ship products, grow revenue, and figure things out together. That only works if everyone feels welcome and respected.

## Our Standards

**Things that make this community great:**

- Being welcoming to newcomers regardless of their technical background
- Sharing what you have learned openly and honestly
- Giving constructive feedback that helps people improve
- Respecting different perspectives and experiences
- Focusing on what is best for the community

**Things that do not belong here:**

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks of any kind
- Trolling, inflammatory comments, or deliberately derailing conversations
- Publishing other people's private information without permission
- Spam, self-promotion disguised as contributions, or low-effort content
- Any behavior that makes someone feel unwelcome or unsafe

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all BootstrapOS spaces: this GitHub repository, pull requests, issues, discussions, the Discord server, and any other community channels.

## Enforcement

If you see behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:

1. **On GitHub:** Open an issue or report it through GitHub's reporting tools
2. **On Discord:** Use Discord's reporting tools or message a moderator directly
3. **Anywhere else:** Reach out via the [BrainyZeiny Discord](https://discord.gg/WeAkRwDf)

All reports will be reviewed and addressed. We will maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct. Repeated violations may result in a temporary or permanent ban from the community.
